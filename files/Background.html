About

Zero Hunger is a complex challenge that involves more than just providing food to people. It requires dealing with political unpredictability, economic inequality, and climate change. Major obstacles include substandard buldings and environmental issue which can have a negative impact on agriculture. In order to address both current needs and underlying issues, the goal needs international suppport and creative solutions.

Sustainable Development Goal 2

Zero Hunger

SDG 2, "SDG 2, "Zero Hunger," aims to eradicate hunger and ensure everyone has access to safe, nutritious food by 2030. It focuses on improving food security, particularly for vulnerable populations, and highlights the need to support small-scale farmers and promote sustainable agricultural practices. By fostering climate-resilient farming and improving food production systems, the goal seeks to build long-term food security and resilience.
